# EvoCoex
Evolution and Coexistence Lab - Raquel F. P. Costa

**Vision Statement**

**EvoCoex Lab (Evolution and Coexistence Lab)**

Graduate School of Education and Human Development, Nagoya University

**Mission**

Our lab investigates direct interactions between humans and nonhuman primates to advance animal welfare, support wildlife conservation, and illuminate the evolutionary roots of human sociality and language. We examine how individual and social context-on both the human and animal sides-shapes the quality, outcomes, and interpretation of these encounters, including moments of mutual understanding as well as miscommunication.

By integrating approaches from primatology, animal behavior, psychology, cognitive science, and anthropology, we aim to foster a deeper, more ethical understanding of human-animal relationships-both in managed settings like zoos and in shared natural landscapes.

**Core Research Themes**

Impacts of human presence and proximity on animal behavior and public education: Analyze stress-related behaviors, coping mechanisms, and behavioral diversity in animals, alongside human visitors' sense of connection to animals and their willingness to approach them.

Communication via Gesture & Facial Expression: analyze the structure, diversity, and intentionality of visual signals in apes and humans during naturalistic interactions.

Cultural Modulation: Comparing Japanese and Western human participants, we test how cultural norms in nonverbal communication influence primate responses-linking human diversity to evolutionary divergence.

**Approach & Methods**

Non-invasive behavioral observation at partner institutions (Japan Monkey Centre, Higashiyama Zoo, Akron Zoo)

Direct observation and High-resolution video coding of spontaneous interactions

Computational analysis: sequence modeling, diversity indices, GLMMs, Bayesian, SNA

**Partnerships & Infrastructure**

T-Gex Fellowship funding for data collection and equipment

Domestic and International collaborators in primatology, communication, and conservation, including Japan Monkey Centre, Higashiyama Zoo, and Akron Zoo.

**Training & Values**

We train students in rigorous, ethical, and interdisciplinary science.

Students will join the ongoing projects and may start their own independent projects.

Regular meetings, debates, and shared resources.

Our lab prioritizes:

Welfare-first protocols

Cross-cultural sensitivity

Public engagement (zoo exhibits, science communication)
